What Are Share Certificates? Complete Guide

23 Jul 2024, 00:00

Share certificates are a type of savings product exclusively available at credit unions. They allow account holders to deposit funds for a set period (called the “term”). In return, credit unions pay a fixed annual percentage yield (APY), called a dividend. Share certificates are insured by the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA). Banks offer a nearly identical savings product, but it is called a certificate of deposit (CD)—CDs also pay interest at a fixed APY for a fixed term.
