Volkswagen Beetle (Famous Photo) - On This Day

16 Jun 2024, 00:00

Historical Context Commissioned by Adolf Hitler as a "People's Car" in the 1930s the Volkswagen Beetle was designed by Ferdinand Porsche and his design team at Porsche. Significantly the car had a rear engine, unusual for the time. Only small numbers were made before WWII halted production. After the war, production soared aided by an iconic advertising campaign "Think small". Nicknamed "the Beetle" it overtook the Model T Ford as the best-selling car in the world in 1972, selling over 15 million.
