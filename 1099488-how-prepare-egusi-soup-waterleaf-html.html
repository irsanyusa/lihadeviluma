    Nigerian Food

    Egusi Soup has a lot of names through all tribes of Nigeria. Hausa people call it Miyan Egusi; Igbos call it Ofe-Egusi; Yorubas name it Efo-Egusi; Calabar people call it Efere-Ikon. Therefore, it`s one of the most notorious soups in Nigeria, which is enjoyed by all the tribes.

    Nigerian Recipes

    Egusi Soup can be prepared in different ways in various tribes. Different flavours can be achieved by adding some interesting elements, like Uzuza, Fluted Pumpkin, Water leaf, Afang or bitter leaf. The best ways to prepare Egusi Soup would be with bitter or Water leaves as the soup tastes best with these ingredients. Egusi Soup can be prepared in two ways:

    Egusi Soup with Waterleaf – Preparations

    Before you start cooking – wash the Waterleaf in a separate ball for several times. It`s an easy procedure – you just need to wash and squeeze the Waterleaf for several times with changing the water intervals. Make sure that your Waterleaf is clear.

    If you want to use bitter leaves for the same recipe – you should also wash them properly. It`s needed to illuminate all the bitterness of the leaf.

    You can also soak your Waterleaf in water before washing. You can also boil the Waterleaf on fire for about ten minutes to prepare it for your Egusi Soup.

    Nigerian Soups Directions - Egusi Soup

    • Cook the meat for fifteen minutes. Remember to season it. At the same time, wash the stock fish using warm water and salt. Drop the fish into the meat pot and boil for another fifteen minutes. If you want – you can boil the fish separately from the meat;
    • Dress the smoked fish and put aside;
    • While meat is cooking, take the white Egusi seed and grind it until it`s smooth. Then put it aside into a bowl and add a little water. Do not make it too watery! Allow the substance to soak until it`s ready to be used. If you want to use the caking method, then beat two eggs and use the white to mould them into balls;
    • Check the meat, if it`s prepared, then pour it into the different pot;
    • Now you can choose two different ways to make Egusi Soup.

    How to Make Egusi Soup – Frying Method

    You may want to fry the soup if it`s needed to make the smell gone.

    • Place a dry pot on fire;
    • Pour four spoons of Palm oil;
    • When the oil is heated – place the Egusi-water paste onto the oil and fry it;
    • Allow it to fry completely (it should look like a fried egg);
    • Put the meat content onto the fried Egusi and allow it to bubble;
    • Add some crayfish and other elements;
    • Add the pumpkin, water leaf, bitter leaf, Oziza leaf or Afang leaf;
    • Stir the content for few minutes;
    • Now you can serve the dish happily.

    How to Cook Egusi Soup – Caking Method

    • Heat the meat stock again;
    • Add one cup of water to the meat if it`s needed;
    • Allow the meat to boil and add some moulded Egusi;
    • Allow the content to simmer for ten minutes without stirring;
    • Add the traditional seasoning ingredients, seasoning cubes, pepper and ground crayfish;
    • Add the dressed smoked fish;
    • Allow the substance to simmer for ten more minutes;
    • Boil the substance until the Egusi forms the “chunks”;
    • Add the vegetable content to the soup and boil for five more minutes stirring it all the time;
    • At the end, you should add Afang leaf, Water Leaf, pumpkin, bitter leaf or Oziza leaf;
    • Your dish is ready for serving!
